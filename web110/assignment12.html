In the "Review Mozilla: What is accessibility?" task, I dived into the fundamental concept of web accessibility. This activity gave me a deep understanding of what web accessibility is all about and why it matters. I learned that web accessibility is all about making websites and online content usable for people with disabilities, like those who are visually or hearing impaired. The resources from Mozilla emphasized the importance of following accessibility guidelines and best practices to create a web that's welcoming to all users. This understanding has reinforced my commitment to considering accessibility in every web project I work on. I want to make sure my websites are user-friendly for everyone, regardless of their abilities.
